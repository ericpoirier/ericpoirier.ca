I'm Eric Poirier. I'm the web development team lead at the [Eclipse Foundation](https://eclipse.org), where I work on the public web infrastructure that supports one of the world's largest open source communities. Most of my days are spent thinking about Drupal, developer experience, and — let's be honest — a lot of AI tooling these days.

## Why I'm building this

I've been meaning to write publicly for years. There's no shortage of things I want to say — about Drupal, about working in open source, about the tools and workflows that actually help. But it's easy to let the work consume the writing time.

This blog is me finally making that trade-off in the other direction. Not a content strategy, not a personal brand exercise — just a place to think out loud and share what I learn with people who might find it useful.

## What to expect

I'll write about what I know and what I'm figuring out:

- **Drupal** — real patterns from production, architecture decisions, things I've gotten wrong and fixed
- **Open source** — what it looks like from the inside of a foundation, the governance, the community dynamics
- **Web development** — tools, workflows, and the craft of building things that last
- **AI** — honest notes on how I'm using it (and where I'm skeptical)

Posts will be irregular. Quality over cadence. If something's worth writing, I'll write it.

Thanks for being here.
